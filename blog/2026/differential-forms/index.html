1Differential Forms

微分形式是流形上实值函数的推广。一个微分 \(k\)-形式给每个点分配一个切空间上的 \(k\)-余向量。

首先，微分形式是任意流形上内蕴的对象，所以可以用来构造流形的微分同胚不变量。不同于向量场，虽然也是内蕴的，微分形式有更丰富的代数结构。由于外积，分级和外导数的存在，光滑形式的集合同时是分级代数和微分复形。Moreover, the differential complex of smooth forms on a manifold can be pulled back under a smooth map, making the complex into a contravariant functor called the de Rham complex of the manifold. We will eventually construct the de Rham cohomology of a mnifold from the de Rham complex.

Because integration of functions on a Euclidean space depends on a choice of coordinates and is not invariant under a change of coordinates, it is not possible to integrate functions on a manifold.

Among differentail forms, those of top degree turn out to transform correctly under a change of coordinats and are precisely the objects that can be integrated. The theory of integration on a manifold would not be possible without differential forms.

Very loosely speaking, differentail forms are whatever appears under an integral sign. In this sense, differential forms are as old as calculus, and many theorems in calculus such as Cauthy's integral theorem or Green's theorem can be interpreted as statements about differential forms. Cartan defined formally the algebra of differential forms on \(\mathbb{R}^n\) as the anticommutative graded algebra over \(C^{\infty}\) functions generated by \(d x^1, \ldots, d x^n\) in degree 1. In the same paper one finds for the first time the exteriror derivative on differential forms. The modern definition of a differential form as a section of an exterior power of the cotangent bundle appeared in the late fourties, after the theory of fiber bundle came into being.

In this chapter we give an intro to differential forms from the vector bundle point of view. For simplicity we start with 1-forms, which already have many of the properties of \(k\)-forms. We give various characterizations of smooth forms, and show how to multiply, differentiate, and pull back these forms. In addition to the exterior derivative, we also introduce the Lie derivative and interior multiplication, two other intrinsic operations on a manifold.

1Differential 1-Forms

Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold and \(p\) a point in \(M\). The cotagent space of \(M\) at \(p\), denoted by \(T^{\ast}_p (M)\) or \(T^{\ast}_p M\), is defined to be the dual space of the tangent space \(T_p M\):

\(\displaystyle T_p^{\ast} M = (T_p M)^{\vee} =\operatorname{Hom} (T_p M, \mathbb{R}) .\)

An element of the cotangent space is called a covector at \(p\). Thus, a covector \(\omega_p\) is a linear function

\(\displaystyle \omega_p : T_p M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}.\)

A covector field, a differential 1-form, or more simply a 1-form on \(M\), is a function \(\omega\) that assigns to each point a covector \(\omega_p\) at \(p\). There are many reasons for the great utility of diff forms in manifold theory, among which is the fact that they can be pulled back under a map. This is in contract to vector fields, which in general cannot be pushed forward under a map.

Covector fields arise naturally even when one is interested only in vector fields. For example, if \(X\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) vector field on \(\mathbb{R}^n\), then at each point \(p \in \mathbb{R}^n\), \(X_p = \sum a^i \partial / \partial x^i |_p\). The coefficient \(a^i\) depends on the vector \(X_p\). It is in fact a linear function: \(T_p \mathbb{R}^n \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\), i.e., a covector at \(p\). As \(p\) varies over \(\mathbb{R}^n\), \(a^i\) becomes a covector field on \(\mathbb{R}^n\). Indeed, it is none other than the 1-form \(d x^i\) that picks out the \(i\)th coefficient of a vector field relative to the standard frame \(\partial / \partial x^1, \ldots, \partial / \partial x^n\).

1.1The Differential of a Function

定义 1.1. If \(f\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) real-valued function on a manifold \(M\), its differential is defined to be the 1-form \(d f\) on \(M\) such that for any \(p \in M\) and \(X_p \in T_p M\),

\(\displaystyle (d f)_p (X_p) = X_p f.\)

Instead of \((d f)_p\), we also write \(d f|_p\) for the value of the 1-form \(d f\) at \(p\). This is parallel to the two notations for a tangent vector: \((d / d t)_p = d / d t|_p\).

We encountered anothe notion of the differential, denoted by \(f_{\ast}\) for a map \(f\) between manifolds. Let us compare the two notions of the differential.

命题 1.2. If \(f : M \rightarrow N\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) function, then for \(p \in M\) and \(X_p \in T_p M\),

\(\displaystyle f_{\ast} (X_p) = (d f)_p (X_p) \frac{d}{d t} |_{f (p)} .\)

证明. \(f_{\ast} (X_p) \in T_{f (p)} \mathbb{R}\), there is a real number \(a\) such that

\(\displaystyle f_{\ast} (X_p) = a \frac{d}{d t} |_{f (p)} .\) (1.1)

To evaluate \(a\), apply both sides to \(x\):

\(\displaystyle a = f_{\ast} (X_p) (t) = X_p (t \circ f) = X_p f = (d f)_p (X_p) .\)
\(\Box\)

This proposition shows that under the canonical identification of the tangent space \(T_{f (p)} \mathbb{R}\) with \(\mathbb{R}\) via

\(\displaystyle a \frac{d}{d t} |_{f (p)} \leftrightarrow a,\)

\(f_{\ast}\) is the same as \(d f\). For this reason, we are justified in calling both of them the differential of \(f\).

1.2Local Expression for a Differential 1-Form

Let \((U, \phi) = (U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) be a coordinate chart on a manifold \(M\). Then the differentials \(d x^1, \ldots, d x^n\) are 1-forms on \(U\).

命题 1.3. At each point \(p \in U\), the covectors \((d x^1)_p, \ldots, (d x^n)_p\) form a basis for the cotangent space \(T_p^{\ast} M\) dual to the basis \(\partial / \partial x^1 |_p, \ldots, \partial / \partial x^n |_p\) for the tangent spacce \(T_p M\).

证明.

\(\displaystyle (d x^i) \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x^j} |_p \right) = \frac{\partial}{\partial x^j} |_p x^i = \delta_j^i .\)
\(\Box\)

Every 1-form \(\omega\) can be written as a linear combination

\(\displaystyle \omega = \sum a_i d x^i,\)

where the coefficients \(a_i\) are functions on \(U\).

To find \(a_i\), we apply the usual trick of evaluating both sides on \(\partial / \partial x^j\):

\(\displaystyle (d f) \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x^j} \right) = \sum_i a_i d x^i \left( \frac{\partial}{\partial x^j} \right) \Rightarrow \frac{\partial f}{\partial x^j} = \sum_i a_i \delta_j^i = a_j .\)

This gives a local expression for \(d f\)

\(\displaystyle d f = \sum \frac{\partial f}{\partial x^i} d x^i .\) (1.2)

1.3The Cotangent Bundle

\(\displaystyle T^{\ast} M := \bigcup_{p \in M} T_p^{\ast} M.\) (1.3)

Mimicking the construction of tangent bundle, we give \(T^{\ast} M\) a topology as follows. If \((U, \phi)\) is a chart on \(M\) and \(p \in M\), then each \(\alpha \in T_p^{\ast} M\) can be written uniquely as a linear combination

\(\displaystyle \alpha = \sum c_i (\alpha) d x^i |_p .\)

This gives rise to a bijection

\(\displaystyle \begin{array}{rl} \tilde{\phi} : T^{\ast} U & \rightarrow \phi (U) \times \mathbb{R}^n, \hspace{3cm} \text{(1.4)}\\ \alpha & \mapsto (\phi (p), c_1 (\alpha), \ldots, c_n (\alpha)) = (\phi \circ \pi, c_1, \ldots, c_n) (\alpha) . \end{array}\)

Under this bijection, we can transfer the topology of \(\phi (U) \times \mathbb{R}^n\) to \(T^{\ast} M\).

Properly speaking, the cotangent bundle of a manifold is the triple \((T^{\ast} M, M, \pi)\), while \(T^{\ast} M\) and \(M\) are the total space and the base space, but by abuse of language, it is customary to call \(T^{\ast} M\) the cotangent bundle of \(M\).

In terms of the cotangent bundle, a 1-form on \(M\) is simply a section of the cotangent bundle \(T^{\ast} M\); i.e., it is a map \(\omega : M \rightarrow T^{\ast} M\) such that \(\pi \circ \omega = \mathbb{1}_M\), the identity map on \(M\). We say that a 1-form \(\omega\) is \(C^{\infty}\) if it is \(C^{\infty}\) as a map \(M \rightarrow T^{\ast} M\).

例 1.4. (Liouville form on the cotangent bundle). If a manifold \(M\) has dimension \(n\), then the total space \(T^{\ast} M\) of its cotangent bundle \(\pi : T^{\ast} M \rightarrow M\) is a manifold of dimension \(2 n\). Remarkably, on \(T^{\ast} M\) there is a 1-form \(\lambda\), called the Liouville form (or the Poincare form in some books), defined independently of charts as follows. A point in \(T^{\ast} M\) is a covector \(\omega_p \in T_p^{\ast} M\). If \(X_{\omega_p}\) is a tangent vector to \(T^{\ast} M\) at \(\omega_p\), then the pushforward \(\pi_{\ast} (X_{\omega_p})\) is a tangent vector to \(M\) at \(p\). Therefore, one can pair up \(\omega_p\) and \(\pi_{\ast} (X_{\omega_p})\) to obtain a real number \(\omega_p (\pi_{\ast} (X_{\omega_p}))\). Define

\(\displaystyle \lambda_{\omega_p} (X_{\omega_p}) = \omega_p (\pi_{\ast} (X_{\omega_p})) .\)

The cotangent bundle and the Liouville form on it play an important role in the mthematical theory of classical mechanics.

1.4Characterization of \(C^{\infty}\) 1-Form

The set of all smooth 1-forms on \(M\) has the structure of a vector space, denoted by \(\Omega^1 (M)\).

引理 1.5. Let \((U, \phi)\) be a chart on a manifold \(M\). A 1-form \(\omega = \sum a_i d x^i\) on \(U\) is smooth if and only if the coefficient functions \(a_i\) are all smooth.

命题 1.6. (Smoothness of a 1-form in terms of coefficients). Let \(\omega\) be a 1-form on a manifold \(M\). The following are equivalent:

  1. The 1-form \(\omega\) is smooth on \(M\).

  2. The manifold \(M\) has an atlas such that on any chart \((U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) of the atlas, the coefficients \(a_i\) of \(\omega = \sum a_i d x^i\) relative to the frame \(d x^i\) are all smooth.

  3. On any chart \((U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) on the manifold, the coefficients \(a_i\) of \(\omega = \sum a_i d x^i\) relative to the frame \(d x^i\) are all smooth.

推论 1.7. If \(f\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) function on a manifold \(M\), then its differential \(d f\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) 1-form on \(M\).

命题 1.8. (Linearity of a 1-form over functions) Let \(\omega\) be a 1-form on a manifold \(M\). If \(f\) is a function and \(X\) is a vector field on \(M\), then \(\omega (f X) = f \omega (X)\).

命题 1.9. (Smoothness of a 1-form in terms of vector fields). A 1-form \(\omega\) on a manifold \(M\) is \(C^{\infty}\) if and only if for every \(C^{\infty}\) vector field \(X\) on \(M\), the function \(\omega (X)\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on M.

1.5Pullback of 1-Forms

If \(F : N \rightarrow M\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) map of manifolds, then at each point \(p \in N\) the differential

\(\displaystyle F_{\ast, p} : T_p N \rightarrow T_{F (p)} M\)

is a linear map that pushes forward vectors at \(p\) from \(N\) to \(M\). The codifferential, i.e., the dual of the differential

\(\displaystyle (F_{\ast, p})^{\vee} : T_{F (p)}^{\ast} M \rightarrow T_p^{\ast} N,\)

reverse the arrow and pulls back a covector at \(F (p)\) from \(M\) to \(N\). Another notation for the codifferential is \(F^{\ast} = (F_{\ast, p})^{\vee}\). By the definition of the dual, if \(\omega_{F (p)} \in T_{F (p)}^{\ast} M\) is a covector at \(F (p)\) and \(X_p \in T_p N\) is a tangent vector at \(p\), then

\(\displaystyle F^{\ast} (\omega_{F (p)}) (X_p) = ((F_{\ast, p})^{\vee} \omega_{F (p)}) (X_p) = \omega_{F (p)} (F_{\ast, p} X_p) .\)

We call \(F^{\ast} (\omega_{F (p)})\) the pullback of the covector \(\omega_{F (p)}\) by \(F\).

Unlike vector fields, every covector field can be pulled back by a \(C^{\infty}\) map. Recall that functions can also be pulled back: if \(F\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) map from \(N\) to \(M\) and \(g \in C^{\infty} (M)\), then \(F^{\ast} g = g \circ F \in C^{\infty} (N)\).

This difference in the behavior of vector fields and forms under a map can be traced to a basic asymmetry in the concept of a function – every point in the domain maps to only one image point in the range, but a point in the range can have several preimage points in the domain.

A question naturally suggests itself. Is the pullback of a \(C^{\infty}\) 1-form under a \(C^{\infty}\) map \(C^{\infty}\)? To answer this question, we first need to establish three commutation properties of the pullback: its commutation with the differential, sum and product.

命题 1.10. (Commutation of the pullback with the differential) Let \(F : N \rightarrow M\) be a \(C^{\infty}\) map of manifolds. For any \(h \in C^{\infty} (M)\), \(F^{\ast} (d h) = d (F^{\ast} h)\).

证明. It suffices to check that for any point \(p \in N\) and any tangent vector \(X_p \in T_p N\),

\(\displaystyle (F^{\ast} d h)_p (X_p) = (d F^{\ast} h)_p (X_p) .\) (1.5)

\(\Box\)

命题 1.11. (Pullback of a sum and a product). Let \(F : N \rightarrow M\) be a \(C^{\infty}\) map of manifolds. Suppose \(\omega, \tau \in \Omega^1 (M)\) and \(g \in C^{\infty} (M)\). Then

  1. \(F^{\ast} (\omega + \tau) = F^{\ast} \omega + F^{\ast} \tau\),

  2. \(F^{\ast} (g \omega) = (F^{\ast} g) (F^{\ast} \omega)\).

命题 1.12. (Pullback of a \(C^{\infty}\) 1-form). The pullback \(F^{\ast} \omega\) of a \(C^{\infty}\) 1-form \(\omega\) on \(M\) under a \(C^{\infty}\) map \(F : N \rightarrow M\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) 1-form on N.

例 1.13. (Liouville form on the cotangent bundle). Let \(M\) be a manifold. In terms of the pullback, the Liouville form \(\lambda\) on the cotangent bundle \(T^{\ast} M\) introduced in example 4 can be expressed as \(\lambda_{\omega_p} = \pi^{\ast} (\omega_p)\) at any \(\omega_p \in T^{\ast} M\).

1.6Restrictions of 1-Forms to an Immersed Submanifold

Let \(S \subset M\) be an immersed submanifold and \(i : S \rightarrow M\) the inclusion map. At any \(p \in M\), the differential \(i_{\ast} : T_p S \rightarrow T_p M\) is injective, one may view the tangent space \(T_p S\) as a subspace of \(T_p M\). If \(\omega\) is a 1-form on \(M\), then the restriction of \(\omega\) to \(S\) is the 1-form \(\omega |_S\) defined by

\(\displaystyle (\omega |_S)_p (v) = \omega_p (v) \quad \operatorname{for}\operatorname{all}p \in S\operatorname{and}v \in T_p S.\)

The following proposition shows that the restriction of 1-forms is simply the pullback of the inclusion \(i\).

命题 1.14. If \(i : S \hookrightarrow M\) is the inclusion map of an immersed submanifold \(S\) and \(\omega\) is a 1-form on \(M\), then \(i^{\ast} \omega = \omega |_S\).

2Differential k-Forms

We now generalize the construction of 1-form on a manifold to \(k\)-forms. We show that locally they look no different from \(k\)-forms on \(\mathbb{R}^n\). We consturct the \(k\)th exterior power \(\bigwedge^k (T^{\ast} M)\) of the cotangent bundle. A differential \(k\)-form is seen to be a section of the bundle \(\bigwedge^k (T^{\ast} M)\). This gives a natural notion of smoothness as a section of the vector bundle \(\bigwedge^k (T^{\ast} M)\). The pullback and the wedge product of differential forms are defined pointwise. As examples of differential forms, we consider left-invariant forms on a Lie group.

2.1Differential Forms

\(k\)-tensor

\(\displaystyle f : V \times \cdots \times V \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\)

\(f\) is alternating if for any permutation \(\sigma \in S_k\)

\(\displaystyle f (v_{\sigma (1)}, \ldots, v_{\sigma (k)}) = (\operatorname{sgn} \sigma) f (v_1, \ldots, v_k) .\) (2.1)

An alternating \(k\)-tensor is also called a \(k\)-covector on \(V\).

\(\displaystyle \begin{array}{rl} \wedge^0 (V^{\vee}) & = A_0 (V) =\mathbb{R},\\ \wedge^1 (V^{\vee}) & = A_1 (V) = V^{\vee},\\ \wedge^2 (V^{\vee}) & = A_2 (V), \quad \operatorname{and}\operatorname{so}\operatorname{on}. \end{array}\)

If \(\omega\) is a \(k\)-form on a manifold \(M\) and \(X_1, \ldots, X_k\) are vector fields on \(M\), then \(\omega (X_1, \ldots, X_k)\) is the function on \(M\) defined by

\(\displaystyle (\omega (X_1, \ldots, X_k)) (p) = \omega_p ((X_1)_p, \ldots, (X_k)_p) .\)

命题 2.1. (Multilinearity of a form over functions). Let \(\omega\) be a \(k\)-form on a manifold \(M\). For any vector fields \(X_1, \ldots, X_k\) and any function \(h\) on \(M\),

\(\displaystyle \omega (X_1, \ldots, h X_i, \ldots, X_k) = h \omega (X_1, \ldots, X_k) .\)

例 2.2. Let \((U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) be a cooridnate chart on a manifold. At each point \(p \in U\), a basis for the tangent space \(T_p U\) is

\(\displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x^1} |_p, \ldots, \frac{\partial}{\partial x^n} |_p .\)

The dual basis for the cotangent space \(T^{\ast}_p U\) is

\(\displaystyle (d x^1)_p, \ldots, (d x^n)_p .\)

As \(p\) varies over \(U\), we get differential 1-forms \(d x^1, \ldots, d x^n\) on \(U\).

By proposition before, a basis for the alternating \(k\)-tensor in \(\bigwedge^k (T_p^{\ast} U)\) is

\(\displaystyle (d x^{i_1})_p \wedge \cdots \wedge (d x^{i_k})_p, \quad 1 \leq i_1 < \cdots < i_k \leq n.\)
\(\displaystyle \omega_p = \sum a_{i_1 \ldots i_k} (p) (d x^{i_1})_p \wedge \cdots \wedge (d x^{i_k})_p\)
\(\displaystyle \omega = \sum a_{i_1 \ldots i_k} d x^{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge d x^{i_k}\)

We let

\(\displaystyle \mathcal{J}_{k, n} = \{ I = (i_1, \ldots, i_k) |1 \leq i_1 <, \ldots, < i_k \leq n \}\)

be the set of all strictly ascending multi-indices between 1 and \(n\) of length \(k\), and write

\(\displaystyle \omega = \sum_{I \in \mathcal{J}} a_I d x^I,\)

where \(d x^I\) stands for \(d x^{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge d x^{i_k}\).

2.2Local Expression for a \(k\)-Form

\(\displaystyle d x^I (\partial_{j_1}, \ldots, \partial_{j_k}) = \delta^I_J = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} 1 & \operatorname{for}I = J\\ 0 & \operatorname{for}I \neq J \end{array} \right.\) (2.2)

命题 2.3. (A wedge of differentials in local coordinates).

\(\displaystyle d f^1 \wedge \ldots \wedge d f^k = \sum \frac{\partial (f^1, \ldots, f^k)}{\partial (x^{i_1}, \ldots, x^{i_k})} d x^{i_1} \wedge \ldots \wedge d x^{i_k} .\)

推论 2.4. Let \((U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) be a chart on a manifold, and let \(f, f^1, \ldots, f^n\) be the \(C^{\infty}\) functions on \(U\). Then

  1. (1-forms) \(d f = \sum (\partial f / \partial x^i) d x^i\),

  2. (top-forms) \(d f^1 \wedge \ldots \wedge d f^n = \det [\partial f^j / \partial x^i] d x^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge d x^n\).

例 2.5. (Transition formula for a 2-form).

2.3The Bundle Point of View

\(\displaystyle \wedge^k (T^{\ast} M) := \cup_{p \in M} \wedge^k (T_p^{\ast} M) = \cup_{p \in M} A_k (T_p M)\)

The set is called the \(k\)th exterior power of the cotangent bundle. There is a projection map \(\pi : \wedge^k (T^{\ast} M) \rightarrow M\) given by \(\pi (\alpha) = p\) if \(\alpha \in \wedge^k (T_p^{\ast} M)\).

If \((U, \phi)\) is a coordinate chart on \(M\), then there is a bijection

\(\displaystyle \begin{array}{rl} \wedge^k (T^{\ast} U) & = \cup_{p \in U} \wedge^k (T^{\ast} U) \simeq \phi (U) \times \mathbb{R}^{\text{\scriptsize{$\left( \begin{array}{c} n\\ k \end{array} \right)$}}},\\ & \alpha \in \wedge^k (T_p^{\ast} U) \mapsto (\phi (p), \{ c_I (\alpha) \}_I), \end{array}\)

where \(\alpha = \sum c_I (\alpha) d x^I |_p \in \wedge^k (T_p^{\ast} U)\) and \(I = (1 \leq i_1 <, \ldots, < i_k \leq n)\).

\(\displaystyle \Omega^k (M) = \Gamma (\wedge^k (T^{\ast} M)) = \Gamma (M, \wedge^k (T^{\ast} M)) .\)

2.4Smooth \(k\)-Forms

引理 2.6. (Smoothness of a \(k\)-form on a chart). A \(k\)-form \(\omega = \sum a_I d x^I\) on \(U\) is smooth if and only if the coefficient functions \(a_I\) are all smooth on \(U\).

命题 2.7. (Characterization of a smooth \(k\)-form). Let \(\omega\) be a \(k\)-form on a manifold \(M\). The following are equivalent:

  1. The \(k\)-form \(\omega\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on \(M\).

  2. The manifold \(M\) has an atlas such that on every chart \((U, \phi) = (U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) in the atlas, the coefficients \(a_I\) of \(\omega = \sum a_I d x^I\) relative to the coordinate fram \(\{ d x^I \}_{I \in \mathcal{J}_{k, n}}\) are all \(C^{\infty}\).

  3. On every chart \((U, \phi) = (U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) on \(M\), the coefficients \(a_I\) of \(\omega = \sum a_I d x^I\) relative to the coordinate frame \(\{ d x^I \}_{I \in \mathcal{J}_{k, n}}\) are all \(C^{\infty}\).

  4. For any \(k\)-smooth vector fields \(X_1, \ldots, X_k\) on \(M\), the functions \(\omega (X_1, \ldots, X_k)\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on \(M\).

\(\displaystyle \Omega^0 (M) = \Gamma (\wedge^0 (T^{\ast} M)) = \Gamma (M \times \mathbb{R}) = C^{\infty} (M) .\)
