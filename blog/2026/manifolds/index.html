1.1Topological Manifolds 拓扑流形

定义 1.1. A topological space \(M\) is locally Euclidean of dimension \(n\) if every point \(p\) in \(M\) has a neighborhood \(U\) such that there is a homeomorphism \(\phi\) from \(U\) onto an open subset of \(\mathbb{R}^n\). We call the pair \((U, \phi : U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n)\) a chart, \(U\) a coordinate neighborhood or a coordinate open set, and \(\phi\) a coordinate map or a coordinate system on \(U\). We say that a chart \((U, \phi)\) is centered at \(p \in U\) if \(\phi (p) = 0\).

定义 1.2. A topological manifold is a Hausdroff, second countable, locally Euclidean space. It is said to be of dimension \(n\) if it is locally Euclidean of dimension \(n\).

1.2Compatiable Charts 兼容图册

定义 1.5. Two charts \((U, \phi : U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n)\), \((V, \psi : V \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n)\) of a topological manifold are \(C^{\infty}\)-compatible if the two maps

\(\displaystyle \phi \circ \psi^{- 1} : \psi (U \cap V) \rightarrow \phi (U \cap V),\)

\(\displaystyle \psi \circ \phi^{- 1} : \phi (U \cap V) \rightarrow \psi (U \cap V)\)

are \(C^{\infty}\). The two maps are called the transition functions between the charts. If \(U \cap V\) is empty, then the two charts are automatically \(C^{\infty}\)-compatible. To simplify the notation, we will sometimes write \(U_{\alpha \beta}\) for \(U_a \cap U_{\beta}\) and \(U_{\alpha \beta \gamma}\) for \(U_{\alpha} \cap U_{\beta} \cap U_{\gamma}\).

定义 1.6. A \(C^{\infty}\) atlas or simply an atlas on a locally Euclidean space \(M\) is a collection \(\mathfrak{U}= \{ (U_{\alpha}, \phi_{\alpha}) \}\) of pairwise \(C^{\infty}\)-compatible charts that cover \(M\), i.e., such that \(M = \cup_{\alpha} U_{\alpha}\).

例 1.7. (A \(C^{\infty}\) atlas on a circle).

引理 1.8. Let \(\{ (U_{\alpha}, \phi_{\alpha}) \}\) be an atlas on a locally Euclidean space. If two charts \((V, \boldsymbol{\Psi})\) and \((W, \boldsymbol{\sigma})\) are both compatible with the atlas \(\{ (U_{\alpha}, \phi_{\alpha}) \}\), then they are compatible with each other.

1.3Smooth Manifolds 光滑流形

An atlas \(\mathfrak{M}\) on a locally Euclidean space is said to be maximal if it is not contained in a larger atlas; in other words, if \(\mathfrak{U}\) is any other atlas containing \(\mathfrak{M}\), then \(\mathfrak{U}=\mathfrak{M}\).

定义 1.9. A smooth or \(C^{\infty}\) manifold is a topological manifold \(M\) together with a maximal atlas. The maximal atlas is also called a differentiable structure on \(M\). A manifold is said to have dimension \(n\) if all of its connected components have dimension \(n\). A 1-dimensional manifold is also called a curve, a 2-dimensional manifold a surface, and an \(n\)-dimensional manifold an \(n\)-manifold.

命题 1.10. Any atlas \(\mathfrak{U}= \{ (U_{\alpha}, \phi_{\alpha}) \}\) on a locally Euclidean space is contained in a unique maximal atlas.

In 1963, Michel Kervaire and John Milnor determined that there are exactly 28 nondiffeomorphic differentiabl structures of \(S^7\).

Dimension 4 is a mystery. It is not known whether \(S^4\) has a finite or infinite number of differentiable structures. The statement that \(S^4\) has a unique differentiable structure is called the smooth Poincaré conjecture. As of this writing in 2010, this conjecture is still open.

2Smooth Maps on a Manifold 流形上的光滑映射

By the \(C^{\infty}\) compatibility of charts in an atlas, the smoothness of a map turns out to be independent of the choice of charts and is therefore well defined. （与坐标系选取无关的概念是好的定义）We give various vriteria for the smoothness of a map as well as examples of smooth maps.

Next we transfer the notion of partial derivatives from Euclidean space to a coordinate chart on a manifold. Partial derivatives relative to a coordinate chart allow us to generalize the inverse function theorem to manifolds. Using the inverse function theorem, we formulate a criterion for a set of smooth functions to serve as local coordinates near a point.

2.1Smooth Functions on a Manifold 流形上的光滑函数

定义 2.1. Let \(M\) be a smooth manifold of dimension \(n\). A function \(f : M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is said to be \(C^{\infty}\) or smooth at a point \(p\) in \(M\) if there is a chart \((U, \phi)\) about \(p\) in \(M\) such that \(f \circ \phi^{- 1}\), a function defined on the open subset \(\phi (U)\) of \(\mathbb{R}^n\), is \(C^{\infty}\) at \(\phi (p)\). The function \(f\) is said to be \(C^{\infty}\) on \(M\) if it is \(C^{\infty}\) at every point of \(M\).

注意 2.2. The definition of smoothness of a function \(f\) at a point is independent of the chart \((U, \phi)\), for if \(f \circ \phi^{- 1}\) is \(C^{\infty}\) at \(\phi (p)\) and \((V, \psi)\) is any other chart about \(p\) in \(M\), then on \(\psi (U \cap V)\),

\(\displaystyle f \circ \psi^{- 1} = (f \circ \phi^{- 1}) \circ (\phi \circ \psi^{- 1}),\)

which is \(C^{\infty}\) at \(\psi (p)\).

命题 2.3. (Smoothness of a real-valued function). Let \(M\) be a manifold of dimension \(n\), and \(f : M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) a real-valued function on \(M\). The following are equivalent:

  1. The function \(f : M \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is \(C^{\infty}\).

  2. The manifold \(M\) has an atlas such that for every chart \((U, \phi)\) in the atlas, \(f \circ \phi^{- 1} : \mathbb{R}^n \supset \phi (U) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is \(C^{\infty}\).

  3. For every chart \((V, \psi)\) on \(M\), the function \(f \circ \psi^{- 1} : \mathbb{R}^n \supset \psi (V) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is \(C^{\infty}\).

定义 2.4. Let \(F : N \rightarrow M\) be a map and \(h\) a function on \(M\). The pullback of \(h\) by \(F\), denoted by \(F^{\ast} h\), is the composite function \(h \circ F\).

A function \(f\) on \(M\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on a chart \((U , \phi)\) if and only if its pullback \((\phi^{- 1})^{\ast} f\) by \(\phi^{- 1}\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on the subset \(\phi (U)\) of Euclidean space.

2.2Smooth Maps Between Manifolds 流形间的光滑映射

定义 2.5. Let \(N\) and \(M\) be manifolds of dimension \(n\) and \(m\), respectively. A continuous map \(F : N \rightarrow M\) is \(C^{\infty}\) at a point \(p\) if there are charts \((V, \psi)\) about \(F (p)\) in \(M\) and \((U, \phi)\) about \(p\) in \(N\) such that the composition \(\psi \circ F \circ \psi^{- 1}\), a map from the open set \(\phi (F^{- 1} (V) \cap U)\) of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) to \(\mathbb{R}^m\), is \(C^{\infty}\) at \(\phi (p)\). The continous map \(F : N \rightarrow M\) is said to be \(C^{\infty}\) if it is \(C^{\infty}\) at every point of \(N\).

注意 2.6. (Smooth maps into \(\mathbb{R}^m\)). In case \(M =\mathbb{R}^m\), we can take \((\mathbb{R}^m, \mathbb{1}_{\mathbb{R}^m})\) as a chart about \(F (p)\) in \(\mathbb{R}^m\).

命题 2.7. Suppose \(F : N \rightarrow M\) is \(C^{\infty}\) at \(p \in N\). If \((U, \phi)\) is any chart about \(p\) in \(N\) and \((V, \phi)\) is any chart about \(F (p)\) in \(M\), then \(\psi \circ F \circ \phi^{- 1}\) is \(C^{\infty}\) at \(\phi (p)\).

The next proposition gives a way to check smoothness of a map without specifying a point in the domain.

命题 2.8. (Smoothness of a map in terms of charts). Let \(N\) and \(M\) be smooth manifolds, and \(F : N \rightarrow M\) a continuous map. The following are equivalent:

  1. The map \(F : N \rightarrow M\) is \(C^{\infty}\).

  2. There are atlases \(\mathfrak{U}\) for \(N\) and \(\mathfrak{B}\) for \(M\) such that for every chart \((U, \phi)\) in \(U\) and \((V, \psi)\) in \(\mathfrak{B}\), the map

    \(\displaystyle \psi \circ F \circ \phi^{- 1} : \phi (U \cap F^{- 1} (V)) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m\)

    is \(C^{\infty}\).

  3. For every chart \((U, \phi)\) on \(N\) and \((V, \psi)\) on \(M\), the map

    \(\displaystyle \psi \circ F \circ \phi^{- 1} : \phi (U \cap F^{- 1} (V)) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^m\)

    is \(C^{\infty}\).

命题 2.9. (Composition of \(C^{\infty}\) maps). If \(F : N \rightarrow M\) and \(G : M \rightarrow P\) are \(C^{\infty}\) maps of manifolds, then the compositite \(G \circ F : N \rightarrow P\) is \(C^{\infty}\).

\(\displaystyle \mu : G \times G \rightarrow G\)

and the inverse map

\(\displaystyle \iota : G \rightarrow G, \quad \iota (x) = x^{- 1}\)

are both \(C^{\infty}\).

例 2.21. (General linear group). We defined the general linear group

\(\displaystyle \operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R}) = \{ A = [a_{i j}] \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} | \det A \neq 0 \} .\)

As an open subset of \(\mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\), it is a manifold. Since the \((i, j)\)-entry of the product of two matrices \(A\) and \(B\) in \(\operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R})\),

\(\displaystyle (A B)_{i j} = \sum_{k = 1}^n a_{i k} b_{k j},\)

is a polynomial in the coordinates of \(A\) and \(B\), matrix multiplication

\(\displaystyle \mu : \operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R}) \times \operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R}) \rightarrow \operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R})\)

is a \(C^{\infty}\) map.

Recall that the \((i, j)\)-minor of a matrix \(A\) is the determinant of the submatrix of \(A\) obtained by deleting the \(i\)th row and the \(j\)th column of \(A\). By Cramer's rule from linear algebra, the \((i, j)\)-entry of \(A^{- 1}\) is

\(\displaystyle (A^{- 1})_{i j} = \frac{1}{\det A} \cdot (- 1)^{i + j} ((j, i) \text{-minor of } A),\)

which is a \(C^{\infty}\) function of the \(a_{i j}\)'s provided \(\det A \neq 0\). Therefore, the inverse map \(\iota : \operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R}) \rightarrow \operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R})\) is also \(C^{\infty}\). This proves that \(\operatorname{GL} (n, \mathbb{R})\) is a Lie group.

2.6Partial Derivatives 偏导数

On a manifold \(M\) of dimension \(n\), let \((U, \phi)\) be a chart and \(f\) a \(C^{\infty}\) function into \(\mathbb{R}^n\), \(\phi\) has \(n\) components \(x^1, \ldots, x^n\). This means that if \(r^1, \ldots, r^n\) are the standard coordinates on \(\mathbb{R}^n\), then \(x^i = r^i \circ \phi\). For \(p \in U\), we define the partial derivative \(\partial f / \partial x^i\) of \(f\) with respect to \(x^i\) at \(p\) to be

\(\displaystyle \begin{array}{rl} \left. \frac{\partial f}{\partial x^i} \right|_p & := \frac{\partial f}{\partial x^i} (p) := \frac{\partial (f \circ \phi^{- 1})}{\partial r^i} (\phi (p))\\ & := \left. \frac{\partial}{\partial r^i} \right|_{\phi (p)} (f \circ \phi^{- 1}) . \end{array}\)

Since \(p = \phi^{- 1} (\phi (p))\), this equation may be rewritten in the form

\(\displaystyle \frac{\partial f}{\partial x^i} (\phi^{- 1} (\phi (p))) = \frac{\partial (f \circ \phi^{- 1})}{\partial r^i} (\phi (p)) .\)

The partial derivative \(\partial f / \partial x^i\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on \(U\) because its pullback \((\partial f / \partial x^i) \circ \phi^{- 1}\) is \(C^{\infty}\) on \(\phi (U)\).

命题 2.22. Suppose \((U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) is a chart on a manifold. Then \(\partial x^i / \partial x^j = \delta^i_j\).

定义 2.23. Let \(F : N \rightarrow M\) be a smooth map, and let \((U, \phi) = (U, x^1, \ldots, x^n)\) and \((V, \psi) = (V, y^1, \ldots, y^m)\) be charts on \(N\) and \(M\) respectively such that \(F (U) \subset V\). Denote by

\(\displaystyle F^i := y^i \circ F = r^i \circ \psi \circ F : U \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\)

the \(i\)th component of \(F\) in the chart \((V, \psi)\). Then the matrix \([\partial F^i / \partial x^j]\) is called the Jacobian matrix of \(F\) relative to the charts \((U, \phi)\) and \((V, \psi)\). In case \(N\) and \(M\) have the same dimension, the determinant \(\det [\partial F^i / \partial x^j]\) is called the Jacobian determinant of \(F\) relative to the two charts. The Jacobian determinantis also written as \(\partial (F^1, \ldots, F^n) / \partial (x^1, \ldots, x^n)\).

3Quatients 商(流形)

Gluing the edges of a malleable squaer is one way to create new surfaces. The gluing process is called an identification or a quotient construction.

The main results of this section give conditions under which a quotient space remains second countable and Hausdorff. We then study real projective space as an example of a quotient manifold.

Real projective space can be interpreted as a quotient of a sphere with antipodal points identified, or as the set of lines through the origin in a vector space. These two interpretations give rise to two distinct generalizations–covering maps on the one hand and Grassmannians of \(k\)-dimensional subspaces of a vector space on the other. In one of the exercieses, we carry out an extensive investigation of \(G (2, 4)\), the Grassmannian of 2-dimensional subspaces of \(\mathbb{R}^4\).

3.1The Quotient Topology 商拓扑

An equivalence relation on a set \(S\) is a reflexive, symmetric, and transitive relation. The equivalence class \([x]\) of \(x \in S\) is the set of all elements in \(S\) equivalent to \(x\). An equivalence relation on \(S\) partitions \(S\) into disjoint equivalence classes. We denote the set of equivalence classes by \(S / \sim\) and call this set the quotient of \(S\) by the equivalence relation \(\sim\). There is a natural projection map \(\pi : S \rightarrow S / \sim\) that sends \(x \in S\) to its equivalence class \([x]\).

Assume now that \(S\) is a topological space. We define a topology on \(S / \sim\) by declaring a set \(U\) in \(S / \sim\) is open if and only if \(\pi^{- 1} (U)\) is open in \(S\). Check the empty set \(\varnothing\) and the entire quotient \(S / \sim\) are open. Further, since

\(\displaystyle \pi^{- 1} \left( \bigcup_{\alpha} U_{\alpha} \right) = \bigcup_{\alpha} \pi^{- 1} (U_{\alpha})\)

and

\(\displaystyle \pi^{- 1} \left( \bigcap_i U_i \right) = \bigcap_i \pi^{- 1} (U_i),\)

the collection of open sets in \(S / \sim\) is closed under arbitrary unions and finte intersections, and is therefore a topology. It is called the quotient topology on \(S / \sim\). With the quotient topology on \(S / \sim\), the projection map \(\pi : S \rightarrow S / \sim\) is automatically continuous, because the inverse image of an open set in \(S / \sim\) is by definition open in \(S\).

3.2Continuity of a Map on a Quotient 商流形上映射的连续性

Suppose a function \(f : S \rightarrow Y\) from \(S\) to another topological space \(Y\) is constant on each equivalence class. Then it induces a map \(\bar{f} : S / \sim \rightarrow Y\) by

\(\displaystyle \bar{f} ([p]) = f (p) \quad \operatorname{for}p \in S.\)

In other words, there is a commutative diagram

命题 3.1. The induced map \(\bar{f} : S / \sim \rightarrow Y\) is continuous if and only if the map \(f : S \rightarrow Y\) is continuous.

3.5Open Equivalence Relations

定义 3.5. An equivalence relation \(\sim\) on a topological space \(S\) is said to be open if and only if the projection map \(\pi : S \rightarrow S / \sim\) is open.

例 3.6. The projection map to a quotient space is in general not open. For example, let \(\sim\) be the equivalence relation on the real line \(\mathbb{R}\) that identifies the two points \(1\) and \(- 1\), and \(\pi : \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}/ \sim\) the projection map. The projection map is not an open map.

Given an equivalence relation \(\sim\) on \(S\), let \(R\) be the subset of \(S \times S\) that defines the relation

\(\displaystyle R = \{ (x, y) \in S \times S|x \sim y \} .\)

We call \(R\) the graph of the equivalence relation \(\sim\).

定理 3.7. Suppose \(\sim\) is an open equivalence relation on a topological space \(S\). Then the quotient space \(S / \sim\) is Hausdorff if and only if the graph \(R\) of \(\sim\) is closed in \(S \times S\).

If the equivalence relation \(\sim\) is equality, then the quotient space \(S / \sim\) is \(S\) itself and the graph \(R\) of \(\sim\) is simply the diagonal

\(\displaystyle \Delta = \{ (x, x) \in S \times S \} .\)

推论 3.8. A topological space \(S\) is Hausdorff if and only if the diagonal \(\Delta\) in \(S \times S\) is closed.

定理 3.9. Let \(\sim\) be an open equivalence relation on a topological space \(S\) with projection \(\pi : S \rightarrow S / \sim\). if \(\mathcal{B}= \{ B_{\alpha} \}\) is a basis for \(S\), then its image \(\{ \pi (B_{\alpha}) \}\) under \(\pi\) is a basis for \(S / \sim\).

推论 3.10. If \(\sim\) is an open equivalence relation on a second-countable space \(S\), then the quotient space \(S / \sim\) is second countable.

3.6Real Projective Space 实射影空间

Define an equivalence relation on \(\mathbb{R}^{n + 1} - \{ 0 \}\) by

\(\displaystyle x \sim y \Longleftrightarrow y = t x \quad \operatorname{for}\operatorname{some}\operatorname{nonzero}\operatorname{real}\operatorname{number}t,\)

where \(x, y \in \mathbb{R}^{n + 1} - \{ 0 \}\). The real projective space \(\mathbb{R}P^n\) is the quotient space of \(\mathbb{R}^{n + 1} - \{ 0 \}\) by this equivalence relation. We denote the equivalence class of a point \((a^0, \ldots, a^n) \in \mathbb{R}^{n + 1} - \{ 0 \}\) by \([a^0, \ldots, a^n]\) and \(\pi : \mathbb{R}^{n + 1} - \{ 0 \} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}P^n\) be the projection. We call \([a^0, \ldots, a^n]\) homogeneous coordinates on \(\mathbb{R}P^n\).

例 3.13. (The real projective plane \(\mathbb{R}P^2\)).

\(\displaystyle \mathbb{R}P^2 \simeq S^2 / \{ \operatorname{antipodal}\operatorname{points} \} = S^2 / \sim .\)

Let \(H^2\) be the closed upper hemisphere

\(\displaystyle H^2 = \{ (x, y, z) \in \mathbb{R}^3 |x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1, z \geqslant 0 \}\)

and let \(D^2\) be the closed unit disk

\(\displaystyle D^2 = \{ (x, y) \in \mathbb{R}^2 |x^2 + y^2 = \leqslant 1 \} .\)

The two spaces are homeomorphic to each other via the continuous map

\(\displaystyle \varphi : H^2 \rightarrow D^2,\)
\(\displaystyle \varphi (x, y, z) = (x, y) .\)

and its inverse

\(\displaystyle \psi : D^2 \rightarrow H^2,\)
\(\displaystyle \psi (x, y) = \left( x, y, \sqrt{1 - x^2 - y^2} \right) .\)

On \(H^2\), define an equivalence relation \(\sim\) by identifying the antipodal points on the equator.

On \(D^2\), define an equivalence relation \(\sim\) by identifying the antipodal points on the boundary circle.

Then \(\varphi\) and \(\psi\) induce homeomorphisms

\(\displaystyle \bar{\varphi} : H^2 / \sim \rightarrow D^2 / \sim, \bar{\psi} : D^2 / \sim \rightarrow H^2 / \sim\)

In summary, there is a sequence of homeomorphisms

\(\displaystyle \mathbb{R}P^2 \xrightarrow{\sim} S^2 / \sim \xrightarrow{\sim} H^2 / \sim \xrightarrow{\sim} D^2 / \sim\)

that identifies the real projective plane as the quotient of the closed disk \(D^2\) with the antipodal points on its boundary identified. This may be the best way to picture \(\mathbb{R}P^2\).

The real projective plane \(\mathbb{R}P^2\) cannot be embedded as a submanifold of \(\mathbb{R}^3\). However, if we allow self-intersection, then we can map \(\mathbb{R}P^2\) into \(\mathbb{R}^3\) as a cross-cap. This map is not one-to-one.

推论 3.15. The real projective space \(\mathbb{R}P^n\) is Hausdorff.

3.7The Standard \(C^{\infty}\) Atlas on a Real Projective Space

We may define

\(\displaystyle U_0 := \{ [a^0, \ldots, a^n] \in \mathbb{R}P^n |a^0 \neq 0 \}\)

Similarly, for each \(i = 1, \ldots, n\), let

\(\displaystyle U_i := \{ [a^0, \ldots, a^n] \in \mathbb{R}P^n |a^i \neq 0 \} .\)

Define \(\phi_0 : U_0 \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^n\) by

\(\displaystyle [a^0, \ldots, a^n] \mapsto \left( \frac{a^1}{a^0}, \ldots, \frac{a^n}{a^0} \right) .\)

This map has a continuous inverse

\(\displaystyle (b^1, \ldots, b^n) \mapsto [1, b^1, \ldots, b^n]\)

and is therefore a homeomorphism. Similarly for all \(i\).

This proves that \(\mathbb{R}P^n\) is locally Euclidean with the \((U, \phi_i)\) as charts.

For intersections \(U_0 \cap U_1\), we have \(a^0 \neq 0\) and \(a^1 \neq 0\), and there are two coordinate systems. We can get transition function

\(\displaystyle (\phi_1 \circ \phi_0^{- 1}) (x) = \left( \frac{1}{x^1}, \frac{x^2}{x^1}, \ldots, \frac{x^n}{x^1} \right) .\)

This is a \(C^{\infty}\) function. On any other \(U_i \cap U_j\) an analogy formula holds. Therefore, the collection \(\{ (U_i, \phi_i) \}\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) atlas for \(\mathbb{R}P^n\), called the standard atlas. This concludes the proof that \(\mathbb{R}P^n\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) manifold.

3.8The Grassmannian \(G (k, n)\)

The Grassmannian \(G (k, n)\) is the set of all \(k\)-planes through the origin in \(\mathbb{R}^n\). Such a \(k\)-plane is a linear subspace of dimension \(k\) of \(\mathbb{R}^n\) and has a basis of \(k\) linearly independent vectors \(a_1, \ldots, a_k\) in \(\mathbb{R}^n\). It is therefore completely specified by an \(n \times k\) matrix \(A = [a_1 \cdots a_k]\) of rank \(k\), where the rank of a matrix \(A\), denoted by \(\operatorname{rk}A\), is defined to be the number of linearly independent columns of \(A\). This matrix is called a matrix representative of the \(k\)-plane.

Two bases \(a_1, \ldots, a_k\) and \(b_1, \ldots, b_k\) determine the same \(k\)-plane if there is a change-of-basis matrix \(g = [g_{i j}] \in \operatorname{GL} (k, \mathbb{R})\) such that

\(\displaystyle b_{i j} = \sum a_i g_{i j}, \quad 1 \leqslant i, j \leqslant k.\)

In matrix notation, \(B = A g\).

Let \(F (k, n)\) be the set of all \(n \times k\) matrices of rank \(k\), topologized as a subspace of \(\mathbb{R}^{n \times k}\), and \(\sim\) the equivalence relation

\(\displaystyle A \sim B \text{ iff } \text{$\text{} \operatorname{there}\operatorname{is}a\operatorname{matrix}$} g \in \operatorname{GL} (k, \mathbb{R}) \operatorname{such}\operatorname{that}B = A g.\)

\(F (k, n)\) is an open subset. There is a bijection between \(G (k, n)\) and the quotient space \(F (k, n) / \sim\). We give the Grassmannian \(G (k, n)\) the quotient topology on \(F (k, n) / \sim\).

Exercieses include open equivalence relation, second countable, the graph \(R\) in \(S \times S\) of the equivalence relation \(\sim\) is closed, and \(G (k, n)\) is Hausdorff.

Next we want to find a \(C^{\infty}\) atlas on the Grassmannian \(G (k, n)\). For simplicity, we specialize to \(G (2, 4)\). For any \(4 \times 2\) matrix \(A\), let \(A_{i j}\) be the \(2 \times 2\) submatrix consisting of its \(i\)th row and \(j\)th row. Define

\(\displaystyle V_{i j} = \{ A \in F (2, 4) |A_{i j} \operatorname{is}\operatorname{nonsingular} \} .\)

Because the complement of \(V_{i j}\) in \(F (2, 4)\) is defined by the vanishing of \(\det A_{i j}\), we conclude that \(V_{i j}\) is an open subset of \(F (2, 4)\).

Similar considerations shows that \(F (k, n)\) has an open cover \(\{ V_I \}\), where \(I\) is strictly ascending multi-index \(1 \leqslant i_1 < \cdots < i_k \leqslant n\). For \(A \in F (k, n)\), let \(A_I\) be the \(k \times k\) submatrix of \(A\) consisting of \(i_1\)th,..,\(i_k\)th rows of \(A\). Define

\(\displaystyle V_I = \{ A \in G (k, n) | \det A_I \neq 0 \} .\)

Next define \(\tilde{\phi}_I : V_I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{(n - k) \times k}\) by

\(\displaystyle \tilde{\phi}_I (A) = (A A_I^{- 1})_{I'},\)

where \(()_{I'}\) denotes the \((n - k) \times k\) submatrix obtained from the complement \(I'\) of the multi-index \(I\). Let \(U_I = V_I / \sim\). Then \(\tilde{\phi}\) induces a homeomorphism \(\phi : U_I \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{(n - k) \times k}\). It is not difficult to show that \(\{ (U_I, \phi_I) \}\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) atlas for \(G (k, n)\). Therefore the Grassmannian \(G (k, n)\) is a \(C^{\infty}\) manifold of dimension \(k (n - k)\).
